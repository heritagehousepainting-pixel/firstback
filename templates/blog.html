Field notes

Short, practical reads on winning the speed-to-lead game and running a tighter home-services business.

The first to reply wins the job

June 10, 2026 · 3 min read

Homeowners rarely hire the cheapest contractor — they hire the one who gets back to them first. Study after study on “speed to lead” says the same thing: respond in minutes and your odds of winning the work jump dramatically. Wait until the end of the day and the job is usually gone.

The catch is that the calls you miss are exactly the ones you can’t respond to fast — you’re on a roof, under a sink, or driving. That’s the gap FirstBack closes: an instant, human-sounding text the moment a call goes unanswered, so “first to reply” is always you.

Stop letting voicemail kill your pipeline

June 3, 2026 · 4 min read

Most homeowners won’t leave a voicemail, and the ones who do expect a callback that often never comes in time. Every unreturned call is a job that quietly walked to a competitor — and you never even knew it existed.

Replacing voicemail with an instant text-back changes the math. Instead of a dead-end recording, the caller gets a real reply, answers a couple of questions, and lands on your calendar — all before you’ve climbed down. The lead that used to vanish is now a booked estimate.

What to put in your AI instructions

May 27, 2026 · 5 min read

The best text-back doesn’t sound like a bot — it sounds like your sharpest employee. Give FirstBack three things: what you do (your trade and service area), what to find out (job type, location, urgency), and how to close (offer two windows, book the one they pick).

Keep it short and human. “Be warm, ask one thing at a time, never sound like a form.” That single paragraph is the difference between a caller who books and one who bounces. Grab a starting point on the Templates page and tweak it in your voice.
