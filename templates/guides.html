Guides

Step-by-step playbooks to get FirstBack live and keep your calendar full. Open one to read it right here.

Set up missed-call text-back in 10 minutes

Create your account, add your business name, trade, and service area, then connect the number you want covered. FirstBack writes a first-draft greeting from your details — tweak it, send yourself a test, and you’re catching missed calls. Most contractors finish this in a single sitting.

Write AI instructions that sound like you

Think of it as briefing a sharp new front-desk hire: tell FirstBack what you install or repair, the questions worth asking (job type, address, urgency), and your booking rules. Keep it short and human — “be warm, ask one thing at a time, offer two windows and book the one they pick.” The Templates page has a starting point for every trade.

Forward your missed calls (by carrier)

You keep your existing number and forward only unanswered calls to FirstBack. On most carriers that’s a short *-code dialed from your phone (conditional call forwarding). We show you the exact sequence for your carrier during setup, and you can turn it off any time.

Set availability that actually books

Add the estimate windows you’re willing to offer, separated by commas. FirstBack only ever offers slots you’ve approved, then confirms the one the caller picks. Keep two or three live at a time so the calendar fills without double-booking.

Handle urgent jobs the right way

FirstBack flags emergency language — flooding, no heat, no power — and notifies you immediately while still replying to the caller. Decide up front how you want to be pinged so the jobs that can’t wait never sit in a queue.

Your go-live checklist

Profile filled in, AI instructions in your voice, calls forwarded, availability set, and one test conversation run end-to-end. Tick those five and you’re ready to let FirstBack answer the calls you miss.
